# Marchward Trademark and Brand Usage Policy

"Marchward", the Marchward logo, and related marks (the "Marks") are common-law (unregistered, ™) trademarks of Marchward. The **source code is open** under Apache-2.0; the **Marks are not licensed** under it. Anyone may fork the code, but no one may use our name to imply a fork is official or endorsed. Give away the code, keep the name. (This policy follows the structure of the community-standard [Model Trademark Guidelines](https://modeltrademarkguidelines.org/).)

## You may, without asking
- State that your project "works with Marchward," "is built on Marchward," or "is compatible with Marchward." These are factual, descriptive references.
- Use the Marks in unmodified form to link to the official project.
- Use the name in blog posts, talks, and docs that discuss Marchward.

## You may not, without written permission
- Name a **fork, distribution, or competing service** "Marchward" or anything confusingly similar. Forks must rebrand (as OpenTofu did from Terraform, or Valkey from Redis).
- Use the Marks in a way that implies official status, endorsement, or affiliation.
- Use the Marks or look-alikes in your company name, product name, domain, or logo.
- Modify the logo or use it as your own.

## Forks
You are free to fork the code under Apache-2.0. When you do, choose your own name and logo, and make clear your project is not the official Marchward. We will happily link credible forks, and we will ask you to rename if the branding is confusing.

## Questions and permission requests
Email team@marchward.ai. We aim to be permissive with community and good-faith use, and strict only about confusion and implied endorsement.
